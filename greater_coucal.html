Greater coucal

The greater coucal or crow pheasant (Centropus sinensis), is a large non-parasitic member of the cuckoo order of birds, the CuculiformesThey are large, crow-like with a long tail and coppery brown wings

Scientific Name of Greater coucal

The scientific name of cuckoo bird is Centropus sinensis.

Diet

The greater coucal is a large bird which takes a wide range of insects, caterpillars, snails and small vertebrates such as the saw-scaled vipers. They are also known to eat bird eggs, nestlings, small vertebrates like lizards and frogs, fruits and seeds

Habitat

Greater Coucals are found in a wide range of habitats including cultivation, woodland scrub, parks and large gardens.

Difference between male and female cuckoo

While Greater Coucal males and females share similar plumage, females are slightly larger, and males are responsible for most parental care.

Voice

Special

The Greater Coucal, also known as the Crow Pheasant, is revered as a symbol of good fortune in India
