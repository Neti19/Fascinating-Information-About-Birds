Weaver bird

Weaver birds are a flock of several families of small passerine birds which are closely linked to the finches. Majority of weaver birds (plumages commonly) are yellow, but there are some in red, black or brown varieties while females are often dull brown colours.

Scientific Name of Weaver bird

The scientific name for weaver bird is Ploceidae.

Habitat

In India, weaver birds, particularly the baya weaver, are commonly found in grasslands, cultivated areas, scrub, and secondary growth, often near wetlands or water bodies, where they build their distinctive hanging nests on trees or palm fronds.

Difference between male and female weaver bird

In weaver birds, males often display brighter, more colorful plumage, especially during breeding season, while females tend to have duller, browner colors. Males also build the nests to attract females, while females primarily choose and line the nests.
