Pigeon

Pigeons are gentle, plump, small-billed birds with a skin saddle (cere) between the bill and forehead.

All pigeons strut about with a characteristic bobbing of the head. Because of their long wings and powerful flight muscles, they are strong, swift fliers

Scientific Name of Pigeon

The scientific name for the common pigeon, often referred to as the Indian pigeon, is Columba livia. 

Diet

Wild pigeons and doves eat a variety of grains, seeds, greens, berries, fruits, and will occasionally eat insects, snails and earthworms.

Habitat

Pigeons live in a variety of habitats, including cliffs, caves, parks, and cities. 

In India, pigeons, including species like the Nilgiri wood pigeon and the pale-capped pigeon, are found in diverse habitats, ranging from moist deciduous forests and sholas in the Western Ghats to urban areas and riverine forests on the plains. 

Difference between male and female pigeon

In Indian doves, specifically the Spotted Dove (Spilopelia suratensis), males and females are similar in appearance, but males tend to have a more blueish head top and may be slightly larger than females.
