Tailor bird

Tailorbirds have short rounded wings, short tails, strong legs and long curved bills. The tail is typically held upright, like a wren

Scientific Name of Tailor bird

The scientific name for the common tailorbird, also known as the Indian tailorbird, is OrthotomusÂ sutorius.

Diet

The Indian common tailorbird primarily eats insects like beetles, bugs, ants, and flies, as well as nectar, small fruits, berries, and tiny seeds.

Habitat

The Common Tailorbird, found across tropical Asia, including India, is a songbird that thrives in a variety of habitats, including open farmland, scrub, forest edges, and urban gardens, often seen flitting through shrubs and low trees.

Difference between male and female tailorbird

While both male and female Indian tailorbirds ( Orthotomus sutorius) appear similar, males develop noticeably longer central tail feathers, especially during the breeding season, while females have shorter tails and less rufous on their foreheads.

Tailorbirds get their name from the way their nest is constructed. .The most distinctive feature of the tailorbird is its unique nest-building technique.

It stitches leaves together using plant fibers or spider silk to create a cradle-like structure for its nest.

The edges of a large leaf are pierced and sewn together with plant fibre or spider silk to make a cradle in which the actual nest is built.
