Kite

A kite is lightly built, with a small head, partly bare face, short beak, and long narrow wings and tail. Kites occur worldwide in warm regions

Scientific Name of Kite

The scientific name for the Black Kite, commonly known as the Indian Kite, is Milvus migrans.

Diet

The Brahminy kite, a common kite species in India, primarily feeds on fish, carrion (dead animals), and occasionally small vertebrates like insects, crabs, and other small birds.

They are also known to scavenge and steal prey from other birds

Habitat

The Indian kite, also known as the Black Kite or Pariah Kite, thrives in a diverse range of habitats, including urban areas, wetlands, riverbanks, grasslands, and even open savannas, often seen soaring over cities and scavenging for food.

Difference between male and female kite

The main difference between male and female Indian kites (black kites) is that females are slightly larger and have a larger wingspan than males, although their plumage is otherwise similar. 

Voice

Special

Unlike others of the group, black kites are opportunistic hunters and are more likely to scavenge. They spend much time soaring and gliding in thermals in search of food.

Their angled wing and distinctive forked tail make them easy to identify. They are also vociferous with a shrill whinnying call.
