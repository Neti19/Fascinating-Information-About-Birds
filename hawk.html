Hawk

Hawks are medium to large birds of prey with sharp claws and hooked beaks, known for their keen eyesight and ability to hunt small animals

Scientific Name of Hawk

The scientific name of the owl is Buteo.

Diet

Hawks, as birds of prey (raptors), are carnivores, meaning they primarily eat meat, including small mammals, other birds, reptiles, and insects.

Habitat

Hawks, as a diverse group of birds of prey, inhabit a wide variety of habitats, including open areas like deserts and fields, mountainous plains, and even tropical regions, with some species adapting to urban areas.

Difference between male and female hawk

In many hawk species, females are noticeably larger than males, a trait known as sexual dimorphism, with females being about 25% heavier and larger.

Special

Hawks are special raptors known for their exceptional eyesight, hunting prowess, and diverse nesting habits, with some species even forming lifelong pair bonds.
