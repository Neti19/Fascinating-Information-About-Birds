Woodpecker

Woodpeckers have a straight, chisel-shaped bill. This is a finely-honed tool for excavating wood. What they’re looking for beneath the surface are insects to eat. A long, thin tongue helps them snatch bugs out of little crevices and tunnels they expose in rotting wood.

Scientific Name of Wood pecker

Woodpecker's scientific name is Picinea which is a part of the family Picidae.

Diet

Woodpeckers mostly collect insects like ants, termites, caterpillars, beetles, spiders, lizards, bird eggs, arthropods, tiny rodents, etc. However, some Woodpeckers prefer to eat fruits and nuts that they also keep in stock for the winter.

Habitat

Woodpeckers are primarily arboreal birds of wooded habitats, thriving in a variety of environments including forests, woodlands, savannas, and even some grasslands and deserts, with some species even found in bamboo forests and scrublands.

Difference between male and female cuckoo

While woodpeckers exhibit sexual dimorphism (differences between males and females), the variations are often subtle, with males typically having more vibrant coloration or a larger patch of red on their heads compared to females.

Both have red crowns, but on males the red color extends all the way down to their long bills.
