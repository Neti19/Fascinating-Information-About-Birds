Vulture

ultures are large compared to other birds. Their bald head and neck serve a useful purpose, allowing vultures to steer clear of infection and tangled feathers when eating decaying meat. A strong immune system allows vultures to eat rotting and possibly infected meat without getting sick.

Scientific Name of Vulture

The scientific name for the common vulture is Gyps indicus .

Diet

Vultures are often attracted to large gatherings of other raptors or scavengers as this usually means that there is a carrion nearby. The Indian vultures generally feed on dead cattle and any leftovers by large predators

Habitat

Indian vultures, also known as long-billed vultures are found in open, wooded country, ravines, valleys, scrub, and deciduous forests, often near human habitations and cultivated areas.

Difference between male and female vulture

Indian vultures, also known as long-billed vultures, are sexually dimorphic, with males being larger than females, and both sexes share similar appearances, with a dark head and neck, and a pale collar

Voice

Special

Vultures are large birds that are scavengers, meaning they eat dead animals (carcasses) and play a vital role in cleaning up the environment by preventing the spread of disease. They have strong eyesight, bald heads, and large wings for soaring
